Pretend MBSE Requirements — Portable Variable Soft-Toss Training Machine

Solution-agnostic requirements and constraints intended to drive MBSE functional decomposition and concept exploration for a softball/baseball soft-toss training machine that provides varied tosses rather than a single repeated toss.

1) Mission and scope requirements

R-MSN-001 (Primary objective)

The system shall deliver a soft-toss ball to a batter to support batting practice.

R-MSN-002 (Practice realism)

The system shall provide a non-repetitive practice experience by varying toss outcomes rather than repeating a single fixed toss.

R-MSN-003 (Stakeholders)

The system shall support use by players and coaches during practice sessions.

R-MSN-004 (Use environments)

The system shall be operable in typical practice environments including grass/dirt fields, gymnasiums, and backyards.

2) Top-level functional requirements

R-FUNC-001 (Ball delivery)

The system shall deliver a regulation softball (or equivalent practice ball) from a machine output location toward a batter’s hitting area.

R-FUNC-002 (Varied toss parameters)

The system shall be capable of varying toss height, speed, and direction/angle across successive tosses.

R-FUNC-003 (Toss location variability)

The system shall be capable of delivering tosses to different locations within a user-defined target region appropriate for batting drills.

R-FUNC-004 (Timing control)

The system shall provide a user-defined toss interval (time between tosses), including the ability to adjust the interval during practice.

R-FUNC-005 (Consistency mode)

The system shall support a repeatable mode where, for a given configuration, toss outcomes remain consistent within a defined tolerance for training drills.

R-FUNC-006 (Misfire prevention)

The system shall prevent or detect common feed/launch faults (e.g., no-toss events, weak tosses, or multiple balls entering the launch path at once) and shall respond in a safe, predictable manner.

3) Performance requirements (measurable targets)

R-PERF-001 (Targeting capability)

The system shall be able to intentionally direct tosses to selected portions of a defined strike-zone-like region after a calibration/setup procedure.

R-PERF-002 (Repeatability under controlled feed)

When operated with a consistent ball feed condition, the system shall achieve shot-to-shot landing-location repeatability within a small tolerance band suitable for batting drills.

R-PERF-003 (Useful operating distance)

The system shall support an operating distance range suitable for soft-toss drills while maintaining practical accuracy in that range.

R-PERF-004 (Cycle rate)

The system shall support repeated toss cycles over a full practice session without performance degradation that prevents normal use.

4) Key constraints

R-CNST-001 (Portability)

The system shall be portable and lightweight enough for a single person to transport and set up for practice.

R-CNST-002 (Environmental robustness)

The system shall tolerate dust/dirt exposure and outdoor weather variability typical of practice environments.

R-CNST-003 (Protective enclosure)

The system shall include protective measures to shield internal components from field conditions and weather exposure.

R-CNST-004 (Cost target)

The system shall be designed to achieve a target end-user price range of $150–$300.

R-CNST-005 (Manufacturability)

The system shall be compatible with a simple, low-cost manufacturing approach suitable for repeat production.

R-CNST-006 (Service life)

The system shall be designed for a long service life under repeated use and typical handling/transport conditions.

5) Interfaces and usability requirements

R-INT-001 (User controls)

The system shall provide user controls for: (a) enabling/disabling operation, (b) setting toss timing, and (c) selecting or enabling variation of toss outcomes.

R-INT-002 (Ease of use)

The system shall be easy to set up and operate by a coach or player with minimal training.

R-INT-003 (User feedback)

The system shall provide basic operational feedback (e.g., ready, active, fault/stop) that is understandable in a practice environment.

R-INT-004 (Power source)

The system shall support a reliable power source appropriate for field and indoor use and shall protect users from electrical hazards.

R-INT-005 (Transport and storage)

The system shall support convenient transport and storage (e.g., compact form, manageable handling features) without requiring disassembly beyond normal stow steps.

6) Safety and assurance requirements

R-SAFE-001 (Emergency stop)

The system shall provide an emergency stop function that immediately transitions the system to a safe state.

R-SAFE-002 (Guarding)

The system shall prevent user contact with hazardous moving elements during normal operation and typical setup/maintenance actions.

R-SAFE-003 (Safe fault response)

In the presence of detected faults (e.g., jam, abnormal operation, control error), the system shall enter a safe state that prevents unintended ball launch or unsafe motion.

R-SAFE-004 (Impact resistance)

The system shall tolerate incidental impacts expected in a practice environment (e.g., stray ball contact) without creating unsafe conditions.

7) Reliability, maintainability, and durability requirements

R-RAM-001 (Practice-session reliability)

The system shall operate reliably for an entire practice session without requiring frequent user intervention to restore normal function.

R-RAM-002 (Maintenance accessibility)

The system shall allow routine maintenance tasks (cleaning, inspection, simple adjustments) using common tools and without specialized equipment.

R-RAM-003 (Dust/weather considerations)

The design shall explicitly address environmental failure risks (dirt, weather exposure) through protective design and maintainable interfaces.

R-RAM-004 (Wear and deformation control)

The design shall control wear and deformation of high-use components such that performance remains within specified tolerances over the intended service life.

8) Verification-oriented requirements

R-VER-001 (Accuracy verification)

The system shall be verifiable by a test demonstrating the ability to deliver balls to selected target sub-regions after setup/calibration.

R-VER-002 (Repeatability verification)

The system shall be verifiable by a repeatability test that measures shot-to-shot variability under a controlled feed condition.

R-VER-003 (Variation verification)

The system shall be verifiable by demonstrating measurable variation in toss height, speed, and direction across a defined sequence.

R-VER-004 (Environmental verification)

The system shall be verifiable by an exposure/ingress test representative of dust/dirt and light weather conditions expected during typical practice use.

R-VER-005 (Portability verification)

The system shall be verifiable by demonstrating transport, setup, and stow operations by a single person using only integrated handling features.

Logical system breakdown (requirements-oriented functional “boxes”)

  1. Ball Handling & Storage Subsystem
    • Store practice balls for repeated cycles
    • Present a single ball per cycle to the feed interface
    • Prevent double-feeds and support clearing of jams
  2. Ball Feed Subsystem
    • Transfer one ball per cycle into the delivery/launch path
    • Maintain consistent feed conditions across operating configurations
    • Support adjustable cycle timing / feed cadence
  3. Ball Delivery Subsystem
    • Impart controlled motion to the ball to achieve a soft-toss trajectory
    • Support adjustable toss speed and usable range
    • Support consistent outcomes for a fixed configuration
  4. Aiming / Direction Control Subsystem
    • Adjust toss direction to deliver balls to different target locations
    • Support variation across tosses without excessive setup burden
    • Provide a stable, repeatable aiming reference after setup
  5. Timing & Control Subsystem
    • Coordinate feed, delivery, and aiming functions per cycle
    • Provide user-selectable toss interval and variation settings
    • Detect faults and command safe-state transitions
  6. Power & Electrical Protection Subsystem
    • Provide reliable operational power in indoor/outdoor practice contexts
    • Provide electrical protection appropriate for user-facing equipment
    • Support safe power-up, power-down, and emergency stop behavior
  7. Structure, Enclosure & Environmental Protection Subsystem
    • Provide a stable base and alignment for all functional subsystems
    • Protect internal components from dust/dirt and weather exposure
    • Support portability, handling, and storage constraints
  8. User Interface & Safety Subsystem
    • Provide intuitive controls and operational feedback
    • Prevent user contact with hazards during operation
    • Provide emergency stop and safe fault response behavior
