Pretend MBSE Requirements — Automatic Door Opening Assist (Retrofit)

Solution-agnostic requirements and constraints intended to drive MBSE functional decomposition and concept exploration for a retrofit system that enables automatic opening of existing interior doors to improve accessibility.

1) Mission and scope requirements

R-MSN-001 (Primary function)

The system shall enable an interior hinged door to open automatically for an accessibility user when requested.

R-MSN-002 (Retrofit scope)

The system shall be designed as an add-on for existing doors that already use traditional door-closer hardware.

R-MSN-003 (Deployment context)

The system shall be suitable for installation on a large number of doors in an institutional campus setting (e.g., classroom doors), not just exterior entrances.

R-MSN-004 (Independence objective)

The system shall improve user independence by reducing reliance on assistance from other people for routine door access.

2) Top-level functional requirements

R-FUNC-001 (Initiate automatic opening)

The system shall provide a user-initiated means to request automatic opening without requiring the user to manipulate a door knob.

R-FUNC-002 (Preferred initiation modality)

The primary initiation interface shall be compatible with push-button actuation appropriate for accessibility users.

R-FUNC-003 (Door opening against existing resistance)

The system shall open the door against the resistance of the existing door closer and typical hinge/friction loads.

R-FUNC-004 (Hold-ready state)

The system shall support a “ready” state in which it is prepared to perform an opening upon request, without unintended actuation.

R-FUNC-005 (Non-interference during closing)

The system shall not impede or materially increase resistance during normal door closing under the door closer.

R-FUNC-006 (Directionality)

The system shall ensure that the automatic opening motion occurs in the intended opening direction and does not reverse the door’s expected behavior.

R-FUNC-007 (Reset/return to standby)

After completing an opening cycle (or after a cancellation), the system shall return to a defined standby state suitable for subsequent use.

3) Performance requirements (measurable targets)

R-PERF-001 (Opening capability baseline)

The system shall be capable of opening doors with an opening force requirement up to at least 11.375 lbf (representative campus door measurement).

R-PERF-002 (Full-open completion)

The system shall open the door to a usable open position sufficient for wheelchair passage under normal operating conditions.

R-PERF-003 (Cycle response)

Upon user request, the system shall begin the opening action within a short, user-acceptable delay and proceed with controlled motion.

R-PERF-004 (Repeatability)

The system shall provide repeatable opening performance across repeated cycles without requiring re-adjustment between typical uses.

4) Key constraints

R-CNST-001 (Cost constraint)

The system shall be designed to be lower-cost than typical electrically-driven automatic door operators, suitable for scaling across many doors.

R-CNST-002 (Installation burden)

The system shall be installable without requiring specialized high-skill labor for each door installation, supporting scalable deployment.

R-CNST-003 (Door hardware compatibility)

The system shall be compatible with common door closer interface geometries and shall tolerate variation in existing door hardware across buildings.

R-CNST-004 (Operational simplicity)

The system shall be simple and intuitive for both accessibility users and general building occupants, minimizing the need for training.

R-CNST-005 (Packaging/enclosure)

The system shall fit within a practical on-door or near-door packaging envelope and shall include an enclosure appropriate for building environments.

5) Safety and assurance requirements

R-SAFE-001 (Controlled motion)

The system shall provide controlled door motion such that the opening action does not create unsafe rapid movement for nearby pedestrians.

R-SAFE-002 (User protection)

The system shall prevent user contact with hazardous moving elements during normal operation and typical interaction.

R-SAFE-003 (Fail-safe behavior)

In the presence of a fault, jam, or abnormal operating condition, the system shall transition to a safe state that avoids uncontrolled door motion.

R-SAFE-004 (Pinch/entrapment mitigation)

The system shall minimize pinch points and entrapment hazards introduced by the add-on mechanism.

6) Reliability, maintainability, and durability requirements

R-RAM-001 (High-cycle use)

The system shall withstand frequent daily use in a public building environment without loss of function.

R-RAM-002 (Hold/standby durability)

The system shall maintain its readiness/standby condition for extended periods without unintended actuation or loss of ability to operate when requested.

R-RAM-003 (Serviceability)

The system shall allow inspection and maintenance using common tools, and replaceable wear items shall be accessible.

R-RAM-004 (Environmental robustness)

The system shall operate reliably under typical indoor building conditions including dust, incidental impacts, and temperature variation within conditioned spaces.

7) Verification-oriented requirements

R-VER-001 (Opening-force verification)

The opening capability shall be verifiable by test on representative doors spanning measured opening force requirements up to the specified baseline.

R-VER-002 (Non-interference verification)

Non-interference during door closing shall be verifiable by comparing closing behavior (time/effort) with and without the installed system.

R-VER-003 (Repeatability verification)

Repeatability shall be verifiable by conducting a multi-cycle endurance test demonstrating consistent operation across repeated open/close cycles.

R-VER-004 (Safety verification)

Safe-state behavior shall be verifiable by induced fault/jam scenarios demonstrating controlled stop and absence of hazardous motion.

Logical system breakdown (requirements-oriented functional “boxes”)

  1. User Request & Interface Subsystem
    • Accept user request to open (accessible initiation)
    • Provide simple status feedback (ready / active / fault)
    • Provide a safe cancel/stop interaction
  2. Door Interaction Subsystem
    • Couple to the existing door/closer interface without permanent door replacement
    • Transmit required action to open the door to a usable open position
    • Avoid impeding normal door closing behavior
  3. Readiness / Holding Subsystem
    • Maintain a defined standby state between openings
    • Prevent unintended actuation while in standby
    • Support reset/return to standby after a cycle
  4. Motion Management Subsystem
    • Manage opening motion to be controlled and predictable
    • Limit motion to the intended operating range
    • Detect and handle jams/abnormal conditions
  5. Mounting, Enclosure & Integration Subsystem
    • Provide mounting features compatible with common door installations
    • Provide an enclosure suitable for indoor public-building environments
    • Support scalable installation across many doors
  6. Safety & Assurance Subsystem
    • Prevent user contact with hazardous elements
    • Provide fail-safe behavior under fault conditions
    • Mitigate pinch/entrapment hazards introduced by the system
  7. Maintenance & Lifecycle Support Subsystem
    • Enable routine inspection and service with common tools
    • Support replacement of wear components
    • Support durability for frequent public-building use

End of requirements set. (Intentionally solution-agnostic; no concept solutions included.)
