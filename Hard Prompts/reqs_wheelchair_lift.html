Pretend MBSE Requirements — Assistive Sit-to-Stand Wheelchair Add-on

Requirements and constraints for a device that assists a wheelchair user in transferring from sitting to standing, emphasizing independence, stability, simplicity of operation, and practical manufacturability.

1) Mission and scope requirements

R-MSN-001 (Primary function)

The system shall assist a wheelchair user in transferring from a seated position to a standing position.

R-MSN-002 (Use contexts)

The system shall support use in home and care-facility environments, enabling frequent transfers throughout a typical day.

R-MSN-003 (Independence objective)

The system shall support independent use by the intended user without requiring a high-strength assistant for normal operation.

R-MSN-004 (Integration objective)

The system shall be compatible with a standard manual wheelchair form factor and shall not prevent normal wheelchair mobility when not transferring.

R-MSN-005 (Portability objective)

The system shall remain usable by a wheelchair-bound person without requiring external setup equipment beyond what is integrated on the chair.

2) Functional performance requirements

R-PERF-001 (Transfer motion)

The system shall create a controlled transfer motion that supports moving the user’s center of mass toward a stable standing posture.

R-PERF-002 (Assistive action at seat)

The system shall apply an assistive action to the seat region to provide a helping effect during transfer, reducing required user effort.

R-PERF-003 (Minimum transfer angle)

The system shall enable a seat/transfer interface rotation of at least 45 degrees from horizontal during the assist phase.

R-PERF-004 (Assistive force capability)

The system shall be capable of providing an assistive force during motion sufficient to materially reduce the user’s required standing effort.

R-PERF-005 (Speed of use)

The system shall complete the assistive transfer motion within a time duration that is practical for routine daily use and competitive with existing approaches.

R-PERF-006 (Repeatability)

The system shall provide repeatable transfer behavior across repeated cycles without requiring recalibration between normal uses.

3) Key constraints

R-CNST-001 (Load capacity)

The system shall support a minimum user load of 185 lb during transfer operations.

R-CNST-002 (Added push burden)

The system shall add no more than 40 N of additional push effort compared to the baseline wheelchair during typical rolling movement.

R-CNST-003 (Packaging envelope)

The system shall fit within an available wheelchair integration envelope with maximum added dimensions not exceeding 18 inches in any single direction beyond allowable integration boundaries.

R-CNST-004 (Standing-moment contribution)

The system shall provide more than 50% of the standing moment required during the transfer, relative to an unassisted transfer.

R-CNST-005 (Cost constraint)

The system shall be designed with low cost as a primary constraint, suitable for broad accessibility across individual users and facilities.

R-CNST-006 (Simplicity constraint)

The system shall be simple and easy to use, avoiding complex setup steps and minimizing required user training.

4) Interfaces and compatibility requirements

R-INT-001 (Wheelchair structural interface)

The system shall attach to or integrate with the wheelchair structure without compromising the wheelchair’s structural integrity during normal mobility and during transfer.

R-INT-002 (User interface)

The system shall include an intuitive user control interface that can be operated by low-strength users while seated.

R-INT-003 (Power interface)

The system shall include a defined power interface suitable for the intended deployment environment(s) and shall ensure safe electrical connections and protection.

R-INT-004 (Mobility compatibility)

The system shall not obstruct wheels, hand rims, brakes, footrests, or other essential mobility and access features of the wheelchair.

5) Safety and assurance requirements

R-SAFE-001 (Stability and confidence)

The system shall maintain user stability throughout the assist cycle and shall not introduce tipping or unsafe oscillation under expected use conditions.

R-SAFE-002 (Structural factor of safety)

Load-bearing structural elements shall meet a minimum factor of safety of 4.5 under specified worst-case operating loads.

R-SAFE-003 (Controlled motion)

The system shall provide controlled motion during transfer, including the ability to stop the motion safely if needed.

R-SAFE-004 (Fail-safe behavior)

In the event of loss of power or a detected fault, the system shall transition to a safe state that prevents uncontrolled motion and maintains user safety.

R-SAFE-005 (Pinch/entrapment protection)

The system shall minimize pinch points and entrapment hazards for the user and nearby caregivers during operation.

6) Reliability, maintainability, and lifecycle requirements

R-RAM-001 (Durability)

The system shall withstand repeated daily transfer cycles over its intended service life without loss of function.

R-RAM-002 (Maintainability)

The system shall allow routine inspection and maintenance with commonly available tools and without specialized equipment.

R-RAM-003 (Serviceability)

Replaceable wear components (if any) shall be replaceable without requiring disassembly of major wheelchair elements.

R-RAM-004 (Cleanability)

Surfaces in user contact areas shall be cleanable, and soft goods (if present) shall be removable or otherwise maintainable for hygiene.

7) Verification and validation requirements

R-VER-001 (Functional demonstration)

The system shall be verifiable by demonstrating a complete assisted transfer from seated to standing for the minimum user load.

R-VER-002 (Angle verification)

The system shall be verifiable by measuring that the transfer interface achieves at least 45 degrees during operation.

R-VER-003 (Stability verification)

The system shall be verifiable by testing that stability is maintained throughout the transfer under expected user motions and operating conditions.

R-VER-004 (Structural verification)

The system shall be verifiable through analysis and/or test that the minimum factor of safety requirement is met for load-bearing components.

R-VER-005 (Usability validation)

The system shall be validated via user trials demonstrating that intended users can understand and operate the controls after minimal instruction.

Logical system breakdown (requirements-oriented functional “boxes”)

  1. Transfer Assistance Subsystem
    • Generate the assistive transfer motion
    • Apply assistive action to the seat/interface region
    • Limit motion to the intended range
  2. User Interface & Controls Subsystem
    • Provide user-initiated start/stop control
    • Provide clear status indication (e.g., ready/in-motion/fault)
    • Provide safe stop capability during operation
  3. Wheelchair Integration Subsystem
    • Provide mechanical attachment and alignment features
    • Preserve wheelchair mobility functions and access features
    • Maintain overall structural integrity of the wheelchair
  4. Load Path & Structural Support Subsystem
    • Support user load during seating and transfer
    • Meet minimum factor of safety requirements
    • Maintain stability against tipping and excessive deflection
  5. Power & Protection Subsystem
    • Provide required operational power
    • Provide electrical protection and safe connection/disconnection
    • Support safe behavior under power loss or fault conditions
  6. Safety & Assurance Subsystem
    • Prevent uncontrolled motion
    • Reduce pinch/entrapment hazards
    • Support user confidence through predictable behavior
  7. Maintenance & Lifecycle Support Subsystem
    • Enable inspection/maintenance with common tools
    • Support cleaning/hygiene requirements
    • Support replacement of wear components (if any)

This breakdown is intentionally solution-agnostic and structured to support MBSE functional decomposition and concept exploration.

Note: This is a pretend requirements set intended for MBSE exercises (requirements, constraints, verification hooks, and logical decomposition), not a final product specification.
